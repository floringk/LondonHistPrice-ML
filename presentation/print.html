The Problem & Why It Matters

Motivation

  • ●London — the most volatile residential market in the UK, with direct macro-economic impact
  • ●Price-to-income: ~4× (1995) → >10× (2006) → GFC → COVID "space race" → high rates 2022–2024
  • ●AVMs (Automated Valuation Models) = scalable, objective, real-time valuation
  • ●Central challenge: data leakage — saleEstimate_* / rentEstimate_* columns excluded from training
  • ●Target: history_price (continuous GBP, heavy-tailed log-normal distribution)

Why Trees Win on Static Holdout

Literature confirms our findings — Grinsztajn et al. 2022 · Shwartz-Ziv & Armon 2022

  • ①Robustness to uninformative features — tree splits ignore noise columns; NNs do not
  • ②Discontinuous boundaries — property prices have hard edges (postcode, catchment, taxes) → NN spectral bias hurts
  • ③No scaling requirements — trees are invariant to monotone transforms; NNs need precise preprocessing
On a single-shot holdout with large calendar shift (1995→2024), boosting clearly beats NNs

PyTorch MLP — Architectural Safeguards

Three critical design choices for heavy-tailed financial data

  • ①Log-target transformation — log1p(y) → near-Gaussian → gradient stability
  • ②Strategic bias initialization — final_layer.bias = mean(log1p(y_train)) → eliminates Initial Guessing Bias
  • ③Log-clipping — clamp(pred_log, min_log − margin, max_log + margin) → impossible to predict £26B prices
  • ●Architecture: dropout(0.2) · AdamW · SmoothL1Loss · early stopping on val RMSE in GBP space

What works with vendor features vs. where the model fails

  • External benchmark saleEstimate_lowerPrice: 350,168 £
  • But: depends on vendor data → not independently deployment-safe

Honest Limitations

  • Segment gate FAIL on: Detached, Semi-Detached, price_band > 1M £
  • MAPE = 33.8% → within-10% rate only 24.3% (below commercial AVM standard)
  • Large calendar shift: train 1995–2015 vs test 2018–2024 (much higher prices)

Conclusions & Key Takeaways

  1. RQ1HistGBR dominates static holdout — R² 0.720, 57.6% RMSE uplift vs naive
  2. RQ2MLP is 38% more stable on walk-forward (CV 15.1% vs 24.4%) → better production candidate under temporal drift
  3. RQ3Bias initialization + log-clipping are critical for PyTorch MLP convergence on heavy-tailed financial data
  4. RQ45-bin classification proxy (accuracy 49.51%) translates regression into accessible language for non-technical stakeholders
  5. ⊕No universal winner — trees for peak static accuracy, NNs for temporal stability in production
